Historical overview

Among other things, the reform age played a major role in the development of transportation: the first steam locomotives and ships were launched in that era. This atmosphere of the 1800s is reflected in the replica of the Kisfaludy paddle-boat steamer, which cruises several times a day between Balatonfüred and Tihany.

The history of Kisfaludy

Three decades after the first steamboat launched on the Danube (1817), the construction of the first steamship on Lake Balaton began in Óbuda Shipyard thanks to a significant influence of Count István Széchenyi and Lajos Kossuth, who supported steamboating on Lake Balaton the most. The ship was named after Kisfaludy, who is also known as the poet of Lake Balaton. It set off its first journey from Balatonfüred on Széchenyi”s 55th birthday in 1846, but was not completely ready at that time.

On October 3 the Pesti Divatlap, a newspaper from the capital reported the following: „This interesting ceremony was announced on the shores of Lake Balaton by a crowd of mortars...a massive crowd from all parts of the countryside invaded the ship on the porch: and after it was consecrated, it descended onto the mirror of the Hungarian sea, accompanied by the blessings of those presented.”

The salon was designed to the taste of Széchenyi’s wife, Crescence: its white and gold interior was determined with mahogany columns and mirrors.

Reconstruction

In 2012, the idea to rebuild the Kisfaludy was born. A significant part of the original blueprints were destroyed during World War II, so some drastically different drawings and a photograph helped the builders. For the age-appropriate effect the paddle drive was a prerequisite in the design. Today they are not driven by steam engines but by three-phase asynchronous electric motors.

The reconstruction of the ship began in Szeged in 2013, then in 2014 the finished pieces of the boat were delivered to Siófok. The assembly of the components, certain mechanical work and the painting of the hull took place on the falcon track in Balatonkiliti.

On July 27, 2014 the ship, which was afloat but not ready yet, was finally launched and driven to Alsóörs, where all the mechanical, electrical and interior design works were continued.

The reborn Kisfaludy ran out of the port of Balatonfüred with passengers on board for the first time on July 6, 2015 at five o'clock in the afternoon.

Interesting facts about Kisfaludy paddle-boat steamer from 1846

  • The steam engine of Kisfaludy comes from the Penn plant in Greenwich, England
  • The original ship also housed a dining room and library
  • It was able to tow a barge with a carrying capacity of 200 tons
  • The ship arrived in Balatonfüred in pieces, which was assembled there with the help of the master builder, Precious, and shipbuilders from Óbuda, Trieste and Komárom
  • After arriving in Trieste by sea, the steam engine and its equipment reached Füred with the help of German horses
